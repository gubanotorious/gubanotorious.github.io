  • Exchanges and Trading
    Financial markets and exchanges are obligated to meet KYC and AML compliance standards.

What is Self-Sovereign Identity?

Sovereign as an adjective is defined as "possessed of supreme power", "unlimited in extent", and "enjoying autonomy", often used in reference to a supreme ruler or king. Self-Sovereign Identity (SSI) is the idea that individuals and organizations have complete ownership of their identity and complete control of their personal data. As we move to a more digital existence, being able to properly implement these concepts has become more important than ever. Some of the key components of making this possible in the digital realm are:

  • Persistence and Portability
    Users must have an independent existence. Identities must be long-lived. Information and services about identity must be transportable.
  • Control and Consent
    Users must control their identities. Users must have access to their own data and any associated claims without the interference of gatekeepers or intermediaries. Users must agree to the use of their identity. Disclosure of claims must be minimized. The rights of users must be protected.
  • Transparency and Interoperability
    Systems and algorithms must be transparent. Identities should be as widely usable as possible.
